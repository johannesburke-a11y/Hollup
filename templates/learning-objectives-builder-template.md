## 🧠 Bloom's Taxonomy Quick Reference

> *Choose action verbs that match the cognitive level you're designing for. Higher levels require lower levels — you can't evaluate without first understanding.*

| Level | What It Means | Strong Action Verbs |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Remember** | Recall facts and basic information | Define, List, Recall, Identify, Name, Match, Recognize |
| **2 — Understand** | Explain ideas in your own words | Explain, Summarize, Describe, Interpret, Classify, Paraphrase |
| **3 — Apply** | Use knowledge in a new situation | Use, Demonstrate, Execute, Implement, Solve, Complete, Perform |
| **4 — Analyze** | Break down information to find patterns | Differentiate, Compare, Examine, Distinguish, Organize, Deconstruct |
| **5 — Evaluate** | Justify a decision or course of action | Assess, Judge, Critique, Justify, Recommend, Prioritize, Defend |
| **6 — Create** | Produce new or original work | Design, Build, Develop, Construct, Compose, Plan, Produce |

> 💡 **Tip:** Most eLearning and workshops should target **Levels 3–4 (Apply / Analyze)**. Level 2 is minimum. Level 1 alone is rarely sufficient for behavioral change.

## 🎯 Objective Writing Formula

**Formula:**
> **[Audience]** + **[Action Verb]** + **[Observable Task / Behavior]** + **[Condition]** *(optional)* + **[Performance Standard]** *(optional)*

**Examples:**

| Example | Notes |
|---|---|
| *"Learners will be able to **write** a prompt for an AI tool that produces a usable first draft."* | Apply level — observable, specific |
| *"Participants will be able to **differentiate** between appropriate and inappropriate use cases for generative AI in their role."* | Analyze level — directly testable |
| *"Employees will be able to **follow** the SOP for submitting a workflow automation request without assistance."* | Apply level — references job performance |

### Step 3 — Objective Quality Check

> *Run each draft objective through this checklist before finalizing.*

For each objective, confirm:

- [ ] Starts with a **measurable action verb** (not "understand," "know," or "appreciate")
- [ ] Describes an **observable behavior** — you could watch someone do it
- [ ] Is **specific enough** to drive a single assessment question
- [ ] Is **achievable** within the scope and duration of the course
- [ ] Is **relevant** to the audience's actual job performance
- [ ] Avoids **jargon** the audience may not know

> ❌ **Weak:** "Learners will understand AI."
> ✅ **Strong:** "Learners will be able to identify three appropriate use cases for AI tools within their current role."

## 📊 Evaluation Level Planning (Kirkpatrick)

> *Decide now how you'll know if the training worked. Planning evaluation at the objective stage prevents the "we never measured it" problem.*

| Kirkpatrick Level | Question It Answers | How We'll Measure It |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1 — Reaction** | Did they like it? | Post-training survey |
| **Level 2 — Learning** | Did they learn it? | Knowledge check / assessment score |
| **Level 3 — Behavior** | Are they applying it on the job? | Manager observation / follow-up survey / performance data |
| **Level 4 — Results** | Did it impact the business? | *(KPI, error rate, efficiency metric — define now)* |

**Target measurement timeline:**
- Level 1: Immediately after training
- Level 2: At course completion
- Level 3: *(30 / 60 / 90 days post-training)*
- Level 4: *(Quarterly / annual business review)*
